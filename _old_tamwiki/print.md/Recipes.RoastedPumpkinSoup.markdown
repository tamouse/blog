<div id="wikitext">

The <span
class="wikiword">[SpicedPecans](http://wiki.tamouse.org?n=Recipes.SpicedPecans?action=print)</span>
make this a most interesting soup. Makes 6 1-1/3 cup servings.

<span id="ingredients"></span>

Ingredients
-----------

-   5 to 6 medium carrots, peeled, coarsely chopped
-   1 large white onion, peeled, quartered
-   2 to 3 cloves garlic
-   1 (3- to 4-pound) pumpkin or butternut squash, seeded and quartered
-   olive oil
-   salt
-   freshly cracked black pepper
-   8 fresh sage leaves
-   4 tablespoons packed brown sugar, divided
-   6 cups vegetable broth
-   <span
    class="wikiword">[SpicedPecans](http://wiki.tamouse.org?n=Recipes.SpicedPecans?action=print)</span>
-   a few sprigs of Italian flat-leaf parsley or cilantro

<span id="directions"></span>

Directions
----------

1.  Heat oven to 375℉.
2.  Line roasting pan with foil.
3.  Place carrots, onion and garlic in pan.
4.  Drizzle with olive oil and toss until coated.^[1](#fn1_1)^<span
    id="fnr1_1"></span>
5.  Sprinkle with salt and pepper.
6.  Add pumpkin quarters to pan.
7.  Spread one tablespoon of brown sugar on each pumpkin quarter.
    ^[2](#fn1_2)^<span id="fnr1_2"></span>
8.  Top with sage leaves.
9.  Drizzle with olive oil.
10. Roast vegetables 45 to 60 minutes, or until pumpkin flesh is soft.
    <div class="vspace">

    </div>

11. Meanwhile, in a 4-quart Dutch Oven bring broth just to boiling and
    remove from heat.
12. Set aside to cool slightly.
    <div class="vspace">

    </div>

13. Remove vegetables from oven.
14. Discard sage leaves.
15. Scoop pumpkin flesh into a medium bowl (there should be about 5
    cups).
16. In batches, add vegetables and pumpkin to blender or food processor;
    blend or process until smooth, adding enough cooled broth to reach
    desired level of consistency.
    <div class="vspace">

    </div>

17. To serve, divide soup evenly among six cups or bowls.
18. Top with <span
    class="wikiword">[SpicedAlmonds](http://wiki.tamouse.org?n=Recipes.SpicedAlmonds?action=edit)[?](http://wiki.tamouse.org?n=Recipes.SpicedAlmonds?action=edit)</span>
    and cilantro.
