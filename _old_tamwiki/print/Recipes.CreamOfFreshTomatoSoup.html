Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoon good-quality olive oil
  • 1½ cup chopped red onions (2 onions)
  • 2 carrots, unpeeled and chopped
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic (3 cloves)
  • 4 pound vine-ripened tomatoes, coarsely chopped (5 large)
  • 1½ tsp. sugar
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • ¼ cup packed chopped fresh basil leaves, plus julienned basil leaves, for garnish
  • 3 cup chicken stock
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • Croutons, for garnish

Directions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot over medium-low heat. Add the onions and carrots, and saut?? about 10 minutes, until very tender. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute. Add the tomatoes, sugar, tomato paste, basil, chicken stock, salt and pepper, and stir well. Bring the soup to a boil, lower the heat and simmer uncovered for 30 to 40 minutes, until the tomatoes are very tender.
  2. Add the cream to the soup. Process soup through a food mill (discarding only the dry pulp that remains) or run soup carefully through a blender. Reheat the soup over low heat just until hot and serve with garnish of basil leaves and/or croutons.
